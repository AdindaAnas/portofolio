Overview

Customer churn costs telecom companies not just lost revenue but also acquisition costs for replacements. This project builds a full analytics pipeline on the Telco Customer Churn Dataset (7,043 customers, 21 features) — from database design to a Power BI dashboard — identifying who will churn, how much it costs, and which retention strategy maximizes ROI.

EDA — Key Findings

  • Churn rate: 26.5% of the customer base
  • Customers on month-to-month contracts have the highest churn rate — they dominate the customer base and represent the biggest at-risk segment
  • Customers with short tenure (0–5 months) are the most vulnerable, especially combined with month-to-month contracts — onboarding is a critical phase
  • Higher monthly charges correlate with greater churn risk, particularly customers using electronic check

Database Design

Built a relational database in PostgreSQL to structure the dataset into normalized tables: customers, billing, services, contract_types, payment_methods, internet_services.

Customer Segmentation — Key Findings

  • High Spenders have the highest churn rate and contribute the largest share of revenue at risk
  • Customers with fewer services are more likely to churn
  • While high-risk customers are individually most likely to churn, the medium-risk segment contributes the largest total expected loss due to its larger population

Retention Strategy Simulation

Scenarios tested across discount rates (5%, 10%, 15%), durations (3 or 6 months), and retention success rates (10%, 20%, 30%).

Best Strategy: Target High Risk + High Revenue customers · Discount 5% · Duration 3 months → ROI: 15.93×

A small segment of high-risk, high-value customers contributes disproportionately to potential revenue loss — making them the most impactful and cost-efficient target for retention intervention.

Power BI Dashboard

An interactive Power BI dashboard was built around 5 business questions:

  1. Who are our customers and what is their overall value?
  2. What factors are driving customer churn?
  3. How do churned customers behave compared to retained ones?
  4. Which customers are at risk and how much revenue could be lost?
  5. Which retention strategy gives the highest return?
